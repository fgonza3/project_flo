OECD Housing Prices

Web Development

Background:

I worked with a team of five on a final project analyzing the changes in the housing price-to-income ratio over the past 10 years, focusing specifically on OECD (Organization for Economic Cooperation and Development) countries. Our goal was to track global trends in housing affordability and highlight significant shifts in various economies.

Goal:

Our objective was to develop a practical tool to assist first-time homebuyers in identifying the most favorable economies for purchasing their first home. This tool utilized OECD data to show the percentage change in the housing price-to-income ratio from 2012 to 2022 across member countries.

View website of map

Functions:

The map features points on each OECD country, allowing users to hover and view the percent change in the housing price-to-income ratio from 2012 to 2022. This ratio, calculated by dividing the house price index by nominal disposable income per person, reflects housing affordability. A table on the right provides additional data, including the 2012 and 2022 ratios and the percent change, with a sorting function to arrange countries by percent change.

The website features tabs that provide users with detailed information on the limitations of the project and the background behind the creation of the web GIS tool. Additionally, a reference tab is included to credit sources that influenced the project and to cite the data used.

Summary:

After mapping the price-to-income ratios for 2012 and 2022, Luxembourg showed the largest positive change, with a 70.42% increase, while Romania had the largest negative change, decreasing by 41.62%, indicating improved housing affordability. Overall, most countries experienced an increased gap between housing prices and income, with fewer countries successfully narrowing the gap.
