Summary:

In Downtown, the implementation of three new zoning laws and the planned Civic Campus redevelopment offer significant opportunities for growth and modernization. The new industrial zones—Mixed Manufacturing and Logistics (MML), Industry and Innovation (II), and Urban Industrial (UI)—are designed to balance traditional and emerging industries, diversify industrial uses, and expand employment centers. However, challenges include the potential reduction of public land and affordable housing. To foster a vibrant urban environment, it is recommended to incorporate publicly accessible areas such as parks, plazas, and community centers into the redevelopment. Supporting a variety of uses, including retail, entertainment, cultural spaces, and public utilities, is also advised. Additionally, promoting economic diversity can help alleviate financial pressures on businesses reliant on the Civic Campus. Potential benefits of these initiatives include enhanced community safety, improved prison facilities, and economic revitalization.
