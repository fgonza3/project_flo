Background:

As part of my capstone project for my degree in Environmental Studies, I interned as a project manager for natural resource management with the Chehalis Basin Partnership. In this role, I was responsible for visualizing pre- and post-collected data from previous years, along with current data gathered by local volunteers. These visualizations focused on the hydrology of Scatter Creek, a vital component of the surrounding ecosystem. Of particular importance was the salmon population, a key species for both the local environment and economy. Understanding how changes in the creek's hydrology affected salmon populations, as well as identifying the restoration efforts needed to increase water flow throughout the summer, was a critical aspect of my work.

Research Question:

What are the Scatter Creek watershed residents' view on beaver dams? What are the factors needed for social acceptance of beaver-related restoration?
